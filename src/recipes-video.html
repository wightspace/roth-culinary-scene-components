Irish Soda Bread Grilled Cheese with Kale Pesto

by: Chef Allysa Adamson

This recipe is a great summer barbecue side dish. It is quick and easy to put together using the Steam Mode, allowing you to prepare multiple ingredients at the same time.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup unsalted butter
  • 3½ cups plus 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour, divided
  • ½ cup old-fashioned oats
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • ¾ teaspoon ground black pepper plus additional for topping
  • 1¾ cups buttermilk
  • ¾ cup currants
  • 1 egg, beaten to blend

Instructions

Position rack in center of oven and preheat to 375 degrees. Stir butter in heavy small saucepan over medium heat until golden brown and starting to smell nutty, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat.

Stir 3½ cups flour, oats, sugar, rosemary, caraway seeds, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and ¾ teaspoon pepper in large bowl to blend. Pour buttermilk and melted browned butter over flour mixture; stir with fork until flour mixture is moistened. Combine the currants with 1 tablespoon of flour and mix into the dough.

Turn dough out onto floured work surface. Knead gently just until dough comes together, about 7 turns. Shape into a round loaf. Place on ungreased baking sheet. Brush top with beaten egg. Using small sharp knife, cut 1/2-inch-deep X in top of bread.

Bake until deep golden brown and tester inserted into center comes out clean, about 45 to 55 minutes. Cool breads on rack at least 30 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature.
